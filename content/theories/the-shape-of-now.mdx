The standard picture treats the present as a vanishing line between past and future. But when you watch experience carefully, the "now" you actually inhabit is wider than that — it has duration, and inside that duration there is structure.

> The present is not a point. It is a small room you are always standing inside.

This matters because almost every problem we have with time — anxiety about the future, grief about the past — assumes a present that is too thin to live in. Widen the present and the geometry of those problems changes.

## What the room contains

Three things, at least: a fading edge of what just happened, a forward-leaning sense of what is about to happen, and the steady hum of attention itself holding them together.
