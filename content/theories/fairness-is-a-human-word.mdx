A storm hits one farm and skips the next. A child is born into a body that works and another is born into a body that does not. The wolf catches the slow deer, not the bad deer. None of this is unjust. None of it is just either. Justice is not in the equation. The equation runs and the outcomes happen and that is the whole event.

> Nature is not unfair. It is pre fair. The category does not apply to it.

Fairness is a human word. We invented it because we are a particular kind of social animal that needs to keep cooperation stable, and the easiest way to do that was to install, in each of us, a strong feeling that certain trades and certain outcomes are owed [1, 2]. The feeling is real. The thing it is tracking is real. But the thing it is tracking is a property of agreements between people, not a property of the universe.

## How rare it actually is

Fairness, as a working idea, exists in a tiny pocket. It exists between people who have agreed to it, more or less, and only as long as the agreement holds. Step outside the pocket and the concept stops applying. Animals do not run on it. Weather does not run on it. Disease does not run on it. Time does not run on it. The vast majority of what happens to you, and to everyone you love, happens in a place where fairness was never an option.

This is not cynicism. It is just zoning. Inside the human pocket, fairness is one of our best inventions. Outside it, asking whether a thing is fair is like asking whether a mountain is polite [3].

## Why it hurts so much

Most of our private suffering is generated by quietly assuming that reality is in the pocket. We assume the world owes us a return on our effort, a punishment for the people who wronged us, a reward for the people we love, a recovery from the illness, a happy ending to the story. None of these are crazy expectations to have inside an agreement. They are crazy expectations to have outside one. And we hold them outside one almost all the time.

The pain is not that the world is unfair. The pain is that we are running a fairness check on a system that does not have the function defined.

## What changes when you see this

You stop arguing with weather. You stop demanding apologies from time. You stop expecting illness to honor your character or luck to reward your effort. You start saving the fairness category for the place it actually works, which is between people who have agreed to be governed by it, and you become much more careful, and much more grateful, about who those people are.

The world does not owe you anything. That is not a tragedy. It is the precondition for everything inside the human pocket actually meaning what it means. Fairness is real because we made it. It is rare because we have to keep making it. And the fact that anyone, anywhere, ever bothers to be fair to anyone else is, against the backdrop of the rest of the universe, an extraordinary thing.
